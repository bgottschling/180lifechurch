# 180 Life Church Website: Content Management Setup

## How This Works

The new website is built with a modern, fast framework (Next.js). To let your team update text, photos, events, and everything else without touching code, we'll connect it to WordPress as your content editor. You'll log into WordPress, make changes, and they'll show up on the live site automatically.

Think of it like this: **WordPress is where you type, the website is what people see.** They're connected behind the scenes.

## What the Editing Experience Looks Like

When you log into WordPress, you'll see a clean dashboard with sections like:

1. **Site Settings** -- Your logo, phone number, address, social links. Change once, updates everywhere.
2. **Homepage** -- Each section of the homepage laid out with simple text boxes, image uploaders, and dropdowns. No code.
3. **Events** -- Works like writing a blog post. Click "Add New Event", fill in the title, date, time, and description, upload a photo if you want, and hit Publish.
4. **Sermons** -- Same idea. Add a title, paste a YouTube link, pick the series, and publish. (Future feature)

Changes go live on the website within a few minutes of hitting Publish or Update.

## How to Find the Info We're Asking For

Not sure where to find some of the things we asked about? Here's a step-by-step guide for each one.

### Finding Your WordPress Login

If you already have a WordPress website, your login page is usually at one of these addresses. Try typing them into your browser:

- `180lifechurch.org/wp-admin`
- `180lifechurch.org/wp-login.php`
- `180lifechurch.org/admin`

If one of those loads a login screen, that's it. Write down the full URL. If none of them work, your site may not be on WordPress, or it may be on a different platform (like Squarespace or Wix). That's fine too, just let us know.

### Finding Your Hosting Provider

Your hosting provider is the company that keeps your website online. If you're not sure who it is, here are a few ways to find out:

1. **Check your email** for receipts or billing notices. Search your inbox for "hosting", "renewal", or "domain" from companies like GoDaddy, Bluehost, SiteGround, WP Engine, HostGator, or Namecheap.
2. **Check your credit card or bank statement** for a recurring charge from a hosting company (usually monthly or yearly).
3. **Ask the person who originally set up the website.** They'll likely remember.
4. **Look up your domain** at [who.is](https://who.is). Type in `180lifechurch.org` and scroll down. The "Name Server" entries sometimes tell you who the host is (e.g., if it says `ns1.bluehost.com`, your host is Bluehost).

### Finding Your Domain Registrar

Your domain registrar is the company you bought `180lifechurch.org` from. This is sometimes the same as your hosting company, but not always.

1. Go to [who.is](https://who.is) and search for `180lifechurch.org`.
2. Look for "Registrar" near the top. That's the company that manages your domain name.
3. If you need to log in there, check your email for messages from that company (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains, etc.).

### Finding Your Social Media Links

We need the direct URLs to your profiles. Here's how to grab them:

- **Facebook:** Go to your church's Facebook page. The URL in your browser's address bar is what we need (e.g., `facebook.com/180lifechurch`).
- **Instagram:** Go to your profile on instagram.com. The URL will look like `instagram.com/180lifechurch`.
- **YouTube:** Go to your church's YouTube channel. Click on your channel name, then copy the URL from the address bar.

### Finding Your Podcast Links

If your church has a podcast on Apple Podcasts or Spotify:

**Apple Podcasts:**
1. Open [Apple Podcasts on the web](https://podcasts.apple.com) or the Podcasts app on an iPhone/Mac.
2. Search for "180 Life Church".
3. Click on your podcast.
4. Copy the URL from your browser. It will look something like `podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/180-life-church/id...`

**Spotify:**
1. Open [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com) in your browser or the Spotify app.
2. Search for "180 Life Church".
3. Click on your podcast.
4. Click the three dots (...) next to the podcast name, then "Share" and "Copy link".

If you're not sure whether you have a podcast, check with whoever records or uploads your sermons. It may have been set up through a service like Subsplash, Buzzsprout, or Anchor.

### Finding Your Online Giving Link

If people can give online to the church, we want to link to that from the website. Here's how to find the link:

1. **Check your current website** for a "Give" or "Donate" button. Click it and copy the URL it takes you to.
2. **Common giving platforms for churches:** Tithe.ly, Pushpay, Subsplash, Planning Center Giving, or PayPal. Check your email for messages from any of these.
3. **Ask your finance or admin team.** They'll know what platform you use and can share the public giving link.

### Finding Your Planning Center Info

Planning Center is a suite of tools many churches use for service planning, check-ins, groups, and more. If your church uses it:

1. Go to [planningcenteronline.com](https://www.planningcenteronline.com) and try logging in.
2. If you use **Church Center** (the public-facing app), your link is usually `180lifechurch.churchcenter.com`. Try that in your browser.
3. The main thing we need to know is: **do you use it, and which parts?** (Services, People, Groups, Registrations, Giving, Check-Ins)

If you don't use Planning Center, that's totally fine. Just let us know.

### Finding Your Sermon Video Links

If your sermons are on YouTube or another platform:

1. **YouTube:** Go to your church's YouTube channel. We need the channel URL (e.g., `youtube.com/@180lifechurch`). If you want to feature a specific sermon, open that video and copy its URL.
2. **Vimeo:** Same idea. Go to your Vimeo page and copy the URL.
3. **Facebook:** If you livestream on Facebook, go to your page, click "Videos" on the left sidebar, and find the video you want. Copy the URL.

### Finding Your Email Newsletter Info

If you send email newsletters to your congregation:

1. **Mailchimp:** Log in at [mailchimp.com](https://mailchimp.com). We can connect a signup form on the website to your Mailchimp list.
2. **Constant Contact:** Log in at [constantcontact.com](https://www.constantcontact.com).
3. **Other tools:** Subsplash, Flodesk, or church-specific tools also work. Just let us know what you use.
4. **Not sure?** Ask whoever sends the weekly or monthly church emails what tool they use.

### Choosing Who Can Edit What

WordPress lets us set different permission levels for different people. Here's a simple way to think about it:

| Role | What They Can Do | Good For |
|---|---|---|
| **Administrator** | Everything. Full control over the site. | Lead pastor, web team lead |
| **Editor** | Edit all content, but can't change site settings or install plugins | Staff members who update content regularly |
| **Author** | Can add and edit their own content (like events) but not other people's | Volunteers who post events or announcements |

Think about who on your team will be making updates, and which role fits each person. If you're not sure, we can figure it out together.

### Getting Photos Ready

For the website to look its best, we'll need a few photos. Here's what works well:

- **Recommended size:** At least 1600 pixels wide for large photos (hero banner, backgrounds). 800 pixels wide is fine for smaller images (ministry cards, about section).
- **Format:** JPG or PNG files work great. Photos from a smartphone taken in the last few years are usually plenty high quality.
- **What we need photos of:**
  - Worship service (for the top banner)
  - Your congregation or community gathering (for the about section)
  - Any ministry-specific photos you'd like to use
  - Your church building exterior (optional, for future use)
- **Tips for good photos:**
  - Natural, candid moments work better than posed shots
  - Good lighting makes a big difference
  - Landscape (horizontal) orientation is usually better for the website layout
  - If you don't have professional photos yet, that's okay. We have placeholders in place and can swap in real ones whenever they're ready.
